# Evidence-Driven Progress and Adaptation

Progress records demonstrated technical behavior, never personality. Each claim cites a module, test, benchmark, code review, oral answer, or journal entry and carries confidence.

Weakness entries include evidence, confidence, recurrence count, proposed reinforcement, and revisit timing. Distinguish a one-off error from a recurring capability gap. Positive capabilities require transfer evidence, not merely one passing implementation.

Future block design reads all progress files and recent reports. It may reorder future modules, insert a focused drill, raise revisit frequency, delay a boss, or adjust hint pacing. It must preserve roadmap capabilities and frozen standards.

Suggested revisit rhythm is after two modules, after eight modules, in a boss fight, and after the related abstraction, adjusted by evidence. Close a weakness only after independent transfer or repeated strong evidence.

Never infer motivation, intelligence, diligence, or personality from code. Record statements such as “strict left-fold explanation omitted thunk growth in M003 oral defense,” not “careless about performance.”
