## API, invariants, architecture, and changed requirement

The frozen API remains unchanged. Each operation follows the custom `List` structure without conversion-based shortcuts. The reverse accumulator represents the processed prefix in reversed order, and append owns recursion over the left operand. Version 1.0.1 clarified the design scaffold without changing the contract; the resulting timing limitation was disclosed and orally confirmed.

## Explanation/oral defense

- Productivity: `mapList` exposes output as corresponding input constructors become available; `reverseList` cannot expose the first reversed constructor until finite input exhaustion.
- Demand boundary: `takeList 0 input` returns `Nil` without inspecting `input`; after one requested element, an unavailable tail remains undemanded.
- Strictness: an unevaluated length accumulator can form a growing chain of additions, causing avoidable retention and final stack pressure.
- Complexity and induction: append consumes only its first operand and returns the second unchanged at `Nil`; therefore cost and structural induction both follow the first list.
